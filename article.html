Ethiopia and Somaliland Sign Historic Memorandum of Understanding for Port Access

By Samuel Getachew
January 15, 2024
2.4K Views
24 Comments

Addis Ababa - In a landmark agreement that could reshape regional dynamics, Ethiopia and Somaliland have signed a memorandum of understanding (MoU) that grants Ethiopia commercial and military access to the Red Sea through Somaliland's Berbera port.

The agreement, signed on Monday by Ethiopian Prime Minister Abiy Ahmed and Somaliland President Muse Bihi Abdi, represents a significant diplomatic breakthrough for landlocked Ethiopia, which has been seeking direct access to sea ports to support its growing economy.

Key Provisions of the Agreement

According to officials from both sides, the MoU includes the following key provisions:

  • Ethiopia will lease a 20 km stretch of coastline around Berbera port for 50 years
  • Ethiopia will establish a naval base in the region to secure its maritime interests
  • Somaliland will receive shares in Ethiopian Airlines as part of the compensation package
  • The two parties will explore the possibility of recognizing Somaliland's sovereignty

Prime Minister Abiy described the agreement as "a win-win for both nations" that would "unlock massive economic potential for the entire region."

"This agreement allows Ethiopia to diversify its access to sea ports while providing Somaliland with economic benefits and potential diplomatic recognition," Abiy said during the signing ceremony in Addis Ababa.

Regional Reactions

The agreement has drawn mixed reactions from regional actors. Somalia, which considers Somaliland part of its territory, has strongly condemned the deal, calling it a "violation of its sovereignty."

Meanwhile, Egypt and Eritrea have expressed concerns about the potential militarization of the Red Sea region. Analysts suggest the agreement could lead to a realignment of alliances in the Horn of Africa.

International observers will be watching closely to see how this agreement impacts the delicate balance of power in one of the world's most strategically important regions.
